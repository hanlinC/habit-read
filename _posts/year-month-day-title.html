“Give me a ride?”

Ben Barclay checked the horse he was driving and looked attentively at the speaker. He was a stout-built, dark-complexioned man, with a beard of a week’s growth, wearing an old and dirty suit, which would have reduced any tailor to despair if taken to him for cleaning and repairs. A loose hat, with a torn crown, surmounted a singularly ill-favored.

“A tramp, and a hard looking one!” said Ben to himself.

He hesitated about answering, being naturally reluctant to have such a traveling companion.

“Well, what do you say?” demanded the tramp rather impatiently. “There’s plenty of room on that seat, and I’m dead tired.”

“Where are you going?” asked Ben.
